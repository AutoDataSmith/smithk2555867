History

The boathouse and dock at Midler Lake House, integral to the historic Grand white Estate, were originally constructed in 1890 as part of the property’s early development by a wealthy Albany merchant family.

Designed to complement the Main House’s Victorian elegance, the boathouse featured sturdy cedar timbers and a pitched roof, serving as a hub for leisurely boating and fishing on Lake George’s pristine waters.

Restoration

By the early 2000s, years of exposure to the elements had left the structure weathered, prompting owners Daphne and Timothy Midler to undertake a meticulous restoration completed in 2024.

The project preserved the boathouse’s original charm, reinforcing its framework, updating the dock with eco-friendly materials, and adding modern amenities like a slip for kayaks and a sailboat, ensuring it remains a picturesque and functional centerpiece for upscale guests enjoying family get-togethers, corporate retreats, honeymoons, and anniversaries.
